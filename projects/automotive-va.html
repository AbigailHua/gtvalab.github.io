Visual Analytics for Automobile Systems Engineering

SUMMARY

After three industrial revolutions from machine-based production systems to digital computers, the world is currently in “Industrie 4.0”, or the fourth industrial revolution. This revolution introduced the concepts of Digital Twin and Digital Thread which have immense potential to transform engineering, manufacturing, and analysis across various industries. Digital Twin is the digital representation of an industrial asset, be it a machine, control, workflow, or system. Digital Thread meanwhile is a record of an industrial asset's entire life-cycle, from its creation until its removal. The Digital Twin and Digital Thread configuration can answer questions across projects and departments in the organization. Users can trace issues to any point, upstream or downstream in the product life cycle (e.g., If a vehicle has an accident due to unintended acceleration, a Digital Thread will enable backtracking the issue right from testing and engineering up until the design phase.

There exist several heterogenous datasets as part of an automotive systems engineering digital thread and twin configuration - Requirements, Failure Mode Effect Analysis, Functional Safety, Quality Control, etc. This multi-disciplinary research leverages these datasets and develops interactive visual analytics systems to help automotive systems engineers to answer specific questions as well as promote exploratory data analysis with an aim to better decision making and eventually safer cars!
